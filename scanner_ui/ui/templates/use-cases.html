Use cases

The Federal Website Scanner was designed to meet the needs of an initial set of federal partners. We worked with partners to understand the types of scanning tasks they wanted to perform and how they wanted to access the results. If you have use cases to contribute please let us know! E-mail site-scanning@gsa.gov or work with us on GitHub.

Code.gov team

Challenge: The Code.gov team needed on-demand access to a list of active code.json files and a method for analyzing those results.

Solution: Use the file scanner to search for the presence of code.json files across all government websites or a subset of sites. Results can be filtered by branch, agency, or domain. Results include characteristics of the code.json files. A code.json dashboard was developed as a proof-of-concept.

Data.gov team

Challenge: The Data.gov team needed on-demand access to a list of active data.json files.

Solution: Use the file scanner to search for the presence of data.json files across all government websites or a subset of sites. Results can be filtered by branch, agency, or domain. Results include characteristics of the data.json files. A data.json dashboard was developed as a proof-of-concept.

Search.gov team

Challenge: The Search.gov needed to analyze the characteristics of an agency's sitemap.xml files.

Solution: A sitemap.xml dashboard was created as a proof-of-concept. The team used the dashboard to filter for the relevant agency, branch, or organization. Results include the URL count and robots.txt locations.

USWDS team

Task: The USWDS (U.S. Web Design System) team wanted to generate a list of domains that have implemented parts of the USWDS.

Challenge:Use the USWDS dashboard to customize a search. Specify an analysis count (such as 50) to filter the results for domains that have implemented USWDS to a certain degree.
