About 10x Site Scanner

Site Scanner is developed through a project with 10x, a GSA program for technology-focused ideas from federal employees.

The vision of this project is to create a way for TTS to offer a low-cost, automated scanning solution so federal stakeholders can determine the best practices government websites are following and identify ways to improve website performance for the public and public servants. We envision an on-demand service that not only reduces the legwork that scanning has historically entailed, but also enriches the data for analyses available. Performance would be measured on a variety of key dimensions like mobile-friendliness, load times, responsiveness. Not only could stakeholders access this critical data, they'd also be able to copy the scan engine and build off of it for their own, customized uses. Our primary focus is on an open, automated, inexpensive, and fast scanning solution.

Here's a historical timeline of web scanning efforts in federal government

* 2011 - 2015: Ben Balter, a staffer within the Office of the Chief Information Officer at the Executive Office of the President and later a Presidential Innovation Fellow, performs an [analysis of federal.gov domains](https://ben.balter.com/2015/05/11/third-analysis-of-federal-executive-dotgovs/). * 2015 - Jon Tindle (OGP), Eric Mill (TTS), and Gray Brooks (TTS) build (a ref:"https://pulse.cio.gov/" pulse.gov and the two open-source site scanners that gather the data for that website: the use of Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure (HTTPS) and participation in the government’s Digital Analytics Program (DAP) . * 2016 - OGP builds Digital Dashboard , which incorporates results from the Pulse HTTPS and DAP scans, as well as accessibility, mobile-responsiveness, IPv6, and Domain Name System Security Extensions (DNSSEC). Federal employees access scan results behind a secure login. * 2015 - 2017 - DHS builds scans for and Trusted Email to help assess whether agencies are in compliance with Binding Operational Directives . From these scans, DHS generates weekly “cyber hygiene reports” and sends these PDFs to agencies. * 2016-2017 - Eric Mill prototypes USWDS, Accessibility, and Third-party Services scans. * July 2018 - Eric Mills submits an idea to 10x to address the inability to easily scan sites for federal website best practices and standards. At the time there is no actively-maintained scanning infrastructure in production, no long-term solution for storing results (cloud.gov sandbox), and site scanning is not architected to be replicable (for interested parties to stand up their own copy of site scans) or extensible (for new, custom scans to be added to the suite). He proposes 10x to build a cloud-based tool that automatically scans federal sites so federal employees could use to see what's behind these sites. * Fall 2018 - 10x funds Site Scanner. Phase 1 begins. Phase 1 team finds in support of the idea and requests Phase 2 funding. * Summer 2019 - 10x funds Site Scanner Phase 2. Team develops initial product strategy, and launches prototypes of several 200 and USWDS scanners and presentation layers. * Fall 2019 - Phase 2 team launches v0.1 of DAP and Third-party Services presentation layers and v0.2 of Data.json, Code.json, /Privacy, robots.txt, /data, /developer, sitemaps.txt, and USWDS presentation layers. Team finds in support of the idea and requests Phase 3 funding. * Winter 2019 - 2020 - 10x funds Site Scanner Phase 3. Team refines product strategy, continues outreach, and develops a customizable UI for Site Scanner.
